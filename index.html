Wong Kar-Wai

Wong Kar-wai, born in Shanghai in 1958, is a renowned filmmaker known for his visually stunning films and unique storytelling. Starting as a screenwriter and assistant director, he made his directorial debut with "As Tears Go By" in 1988. His breakthrough came with "Days of Being Wild" (1990), followed by acclaimed works like "Chungking Express" (1994) and "In the Mood for Love" (2000). Wong's films explore themes of love, longing, and identity, earning him international recognition and establishing himself as a cinematic master.
